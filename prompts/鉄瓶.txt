========================================

Classify the item into one of four values before proceeding. Output the classification before any listing content.

SECTION 5: WORKSHOP / MAKER / FOUNDER HIERARCHY (6 levels)
========================================

Iron kettle workshop attribution requires explicit evidence. VERY HIGH counterfeit risk for 龍文堂 / 亀文堂 / 金壽堂.

Workshop Expression 6 levels (W1-W6):

W1: [Workshop] workshop
  Conditions: maker's mark on BOTH body AND matching lid interior + 共箱 + expert 鑑定書 or 折紙
  龍文堂/亀文堂/金壽堂 RESTRICTION: W1 requires all three forms of evidence above
  Example: Japanese Iron Tetsubin Ryubundo Workshop Meiji

W2: attributed to [Workshop]
  Conditions: maker's mark on body + matching 共箱; expert document not available
  VERY HIGH counterfeit risk workshops (龍文堂/亀文堂/金壽堂): W2 is realistic ceiling without full documentation
  Example: Japanese Iron Tetsubin Attributed to Ryubundo Meiji

W3: with [Workshop] tomobako
  Conditions: 共箱 present but body mark not confirmed or not matching
  Note: box and body may not be original pair — do NOT conflate
  Example: Japanese Iron Tetsubin with Ryubundo Tomobako

W4: bearing [Workshop] mark / seal / kao
  Conditions: mark or seal confirmed on body (not lid only); no matching box; no certificate
  Example: Japanese Iron Tetsubin Bearing Ryubundo Mark Unsigned

W5: marked [Workshop] (lid only)
  Conditions: inscription visible on lid interior only; body mark not confirmed
  Note: lid-only mark is insufficient for Ryubundo main-studio attribution; state explicitly as lid mark only; do NOT combine with body attribution
  Example: Japanese Iron Tetsubin Lid Marked Ryubundo Antique

W6: in the style of [Workshop]
  Conditions: source explicitly uses style/influence language; no workshop claim
  Example: Japanese Iron Tetsubin Ryubundo Style Arare Pattern

Individual Maker / Generation rules:
